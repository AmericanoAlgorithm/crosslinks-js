Purpose

Crosslinks is the MIT study site for students, by students.

When you're frustrated, tired of searching Google endlessly for answers — don't. Come here instead.

Five Facets of Learning

Crosslinks connects topics you learn as an undergrad at MIT. These connections are called crosslinks. If you're familiar with graph theory, you can think of Crosslinks as a graph: topics are nodes on the graph, and connections among topics are directed edges. Every topic page has aggregated links to good materials that MIT students have found helpful.

Learn

The Learn section contains links to material that helps you learn about the topic. Look here if you want to find examples, notes, solutions, etc.

Prepare

The Prepare section contains prerequisite topics. For example, if you're having trouble understanding the Second fundamental theorem of calculus, you should probably go to the Prepare section and review what an Antiderivative is.

Relate

The Relate section contains topics that are closely related, e.g. Bode plots and Nyquist plots.

Advance

The Advance section contains topics that 'come after' the current topic.

Apply

The Apply section points to links that show real-life, interesting applications of this topic.
